# Medium Article Template System

A comprehensive English article template for Medium, mirrored from the Japanese note.com template.
Articles follow a problem-first structure: lead with the reader's pain, not self-promotion.

### Opening Hook Patterns

Use one of these proven openers. Never start with "Hi, I'm..." or "In this article..."

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Scenario** | "It's 4 PM on Friday. Your team just finished a 90-minute meeting, and nobody took notes." |
| **Statistic** | "The average company spends 31 hours per month in unproductive meetings." |
| **Provocative question** | "What if every PowerPoint in your company could become a training video overnight?" |
| **Contrarian take** | "Most AI meeting tools create more work, not less. Here's why." |
| **Before/After** | "Last year, creating one training video took our client 3 weeks. Now it takes 20 minutes." |

### CTA Structure: 3-Tier

Every article ends with three tiers of engagement, ordered from lowest to highest commitment:

1. **Try** (self-serve) — Link to free trial, demo page, or downloadable resource
2. **Attend** (guided) — Webinar registration, live demo session, office hours
3. **Contact** (high-touch) — Enterprise demo request, consultation booking

## 3. Title Transformation Patterns

### Japanese Note Title to English Medium Title

| Japanese note title | English Medium title | Pattern used |
|---|---|---|
| AI議事録、作って終わりになっていませんか？ | "Your AI Meeting Notes Are Useless Without This One Feature" | Provocative claim + curiosity gap |
| PowerPointを入れるだけで動画化できる時代になりました | "Your Company Already Has a Video Studio. It's Called PowerPoint." | Reframe + reveal |
| Excelの手作業、まだ続けますか？ | "The Hidden Cost of Manual Excel Work (We Calculated It)" | Quantified pain + parenthetical hook |
| RPAの移行、実は自動化できます | "RPA Migration Doesn't Have to Be Manual. Here's the Automated Path." | Contrarian + promise |
| 人の目だけで品質管理を続ける限界 | "Human-Only QA Doesn't Scale. Here's What Does." | Limitation + alternative |
| 建設業のバックオフィスが重い本当の理由 | "Why Construction Back-Offices Stay Broken (It's Not a Technology Problem)" | Root cause + reframe |

### SEO-Friendly Title Formulas

Use these patterns. Keep titles under 70 characters when possible.

1. **How to [Outcome] [Constraint]**
   - "How to Turn 500 PowerPoint Files into Training Videos Overnight"

2. **The [Adjective] [Thing] That [Surprising Outcome]**
   - "The Simple Workflow That Eliminated Our Meeting Minutes Backlog"

3. **Why [Common Approach] [Fails/Falls Short] (And What to Do Instead)**
   - "Why AI Transcription Alone Won't Fix Your Meeting Problem"

4. **[Number] [Things] [Audience] [Should/Need to] [Know/Do] About [Topic]**
   - "5 Things L&D Teams Need to Know Before Automating Training Videos"

5. **[Provocative Statement]. Here's [What/Why/How].**
   - "Your DX Initiative Already Failed. Here's How to Actually Fix It."

6. **Stop [Bad Practice]. Start [Good Practice].**
   - "Stop Outsourcing Training Videos. Start Converting Your PowerPoints."

## 4. Cross-Posting Strategy

### YouTube to Medium Article Flow

```
YouTube video published
        │
        ├──► Japanese note article (same week, Mon/Thu)
        │         └── Uses note-video-article-template.md
        │
        └──► English Medium article (same week, Wednesday)
                  └── Uses this template (medium-article-template.md)
                  └── Embeds YouTube video
                  └── Adds 500-1000 words of written context
```

### YouTube Video Embedding in Medium

Medium supports YouTube embeds natively. Paste the full YouTube URL on its own line:

```
## Watch the Full Walkthrough

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VIDEO_ID

*Prefer reading? The full breakdown continues below.*
```

Place the video embed after the problem statement and before the detailed walkthrough.
Always provide the written version below so the article stands on its own.

### Republishing Guidelines

| Scenario | Rule |
|---|---|
| Medium original | Publish on Medium first. Cross-post summary to LinkedIn 48 hours later. |
| YouTube companion | Medium article is the "canonical written version." Link from YouTube description. |
| Note.com adaptation | Medium version is NOT a translation. Rewrite for English audience, different examples. |
| Gumroad paid content | Publish a teaser/excerpt on Medium (first 30%). Link to Gumroad for full PDF. |

**Canonical URL policy:**
- If the content appeared on your own blog first, set the canonical URL in Medium's import tool.
- For Medium-original content, Medium is the canonical source.
- Never publish identical content on Medium and another platform without canonical URLs.

## The Problem

Your company has 500 PowerPoint presentations. Maybe more. Onboarding decks,
compliance training, product walkthroughs, safety procedures. Each one was
carefully created by someone who has since moved on, retired, or forgotten
the context entirely.

Now HR needs these as videos. Remote teams can't sit through live slide
presentations. New hires need self-paced content. Regulators want proof that
training was delivered and completed.

The traditional path: hire a video production agency. Cost: $2,000-5,000 per
video. Timeline: 4-6 weeks per batch. For 500 files, you're looking at
$1M+ and years of work.

Nobody has that budget. So the slides keep collecting dust.

## Why Current Solutions Fall Short

**Screen recording tools** (Loom, OBS): Require someone to narrate each deck
live. At 15 minutes per presentation, 500 decks = 125 hours of recording
alone — before editing.

**AI video generators** (Synthesia, HeyGen): Built for marketing videos, not
bulk training content. No batch processing. No slide-aware formatting.
Pricing per video makes bulk conversion prohibitive.

**Manual video editing** (Premiere, Final Cut): Overkill. You don't need
cinematic effects. You need consistent, narrated walkthroughs at scale.

## A Better Approach: Slide-Aware Batch Conversion

The key insight is that PowerPoint files already contain everything a training
video needs:

- **Visual content** — the slides themselves
- **Narrative structure** — speaker notes (if populated) or slide text
- **Pacing** — slide count and content density
- **Branding** — templates, fonts, colors

What's missing is a narration layer and a video render pipeline that can
process hundreds of files without human intervention.

Training Studio (INMV) treats each PPTX as a video screenplay. It reads the
slide content, generates AI narration from speaker notes or slide text,
applies timing rules, and renders MP4 output — in batch.

## SEO and Medium Tags Strategy

### Tag Selection Rules

Medium allows up to 5 tags per article. Choose tags using this priority:

1. **One high-volume tag** — a broad category with large Medium followership
   (e.g., "Artificial Intelligence", "Productivity", "Corporate Training")
2. **One product-specific tag** — matches what the target buyer would search for
   (e.g., "Training Video", "Meeting Notes", "PowerPoint")
3. **One industry tag** — narrows to the right audience
   (e.g., "Human Resources", "Enterprise Software", "Construction")
4. **One methodology tag** — for thought leadership discoverability
   (e.g., "Digital Transformation", "Workflow Automation", "Business Process")
5. **One trending/timely tag** — if relevant to current discourse
   (e.g., "AI Tools", "Future of Work", "Remote Work")
